About

For the first eighteen years of my life I lived near a harbor town on
Lake Michigan named Ludington (population ~10,000). I attended
the same school district for twelve years, and in May of 2014
I graduated from there alongside 85 other students.

I'm hoping these small numbers resonate with whomever is reading, because they're important to understanding my journey to Computer Science.

Less than 10% of high schools in the U.S. are certified to teach Computer Science- my school was certainly not one of them. No one I knew or spoke with during my years in primary school had ever touched coding, and I was too intimidated to ever dig deeper than MySpace HTML/CSS (haha).

Passion for technology has always been prevalent in my life. Senior year I was struggling to find a major that could stimulate my creativity but also provide financial stability. I love music (bass guitar & percussion) and theatre, but neither have viable job growth. That was when I found Computer Science- a degree that compliments years of technological growth I experienced from middle school to high school and continue to experience today.

The next thing I knew, I ended up at Michigan State University- Go GREEN. I'm currently in my sophomore year pursuing a B.S. in Computer Science. I've finally started to get my feet wet with programming. I know Python, some JavaScript, and am competent in HTML/CSS.

More is soon to come!
